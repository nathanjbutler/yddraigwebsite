    ANNOUNCEMENT

    Join us at the UK's largest ever Viking Festival next June, for a mammoth one-off event that will showcase Viking re-enactors from around the UK and the world. Go back in time as we recreate the Viking age with craftsman, traders, longships and warriors. Witness The Battle of a Thousand Spears, the largest Viking-era battle in the UK for 1000 years. Schools are invited to participate on Friday's Education day. For more information and ticket purchasing please visit: www.vikingfestival.org.uk

    Meet Elina Tomasdottir, she's from the Viking longphort of Annagassan in Ireland, married to Jarl Konal of Mann, horsemaster and musician.

    3 / 30

    Meet Yngvar Eriksson of east angle. His father was a boat builder and moved to Dublin to ply his trade. He grew up on the docks and decided he didn't want to join the family business and joined a ships crew for a life of adventure. He is now a seasoned warrior

    4 / 30

    Sven the Short. Norwegian fishermen who got blown off course while fishing. They eventually landed on the east coast of Mercia, where they decided they didn't want to be fishermen anymore - they'd rather go on an adventure and become great warriors, and started to make their way across Mercia until they reached the Irish Sea. Deciding to see what was across it, they stole a boat, and ended up at the Isle of Mann, where they joined Jarl Brodir, which finally allowed them to be the proper warriors they always wanted to be.

    5 / 30

    Meet Konal Ospakr Ulfrekson from Vannin (Isle of Man). His father a Jarl farms a Quarterland on the island and keeps many longships. He is am married to Elina daughter of one of his fathers Irish allies. He steered his fathers longships for many years. Recently he have been abroad at court in Norway. In his absence his brother Brodir has assumed the Jarldom. He is a warrior, mercenary and trader in people and goods and has contacts from Birka to Dublin.

    Meet Ulfar Sveinson, his parents were farmers in Denmark and he was a second born son who needed to go raiding if he was ever to find wealth and land. He travelled to Dublin in pursuit of this, where he found Jarl Brodir and became an oarsman/warrior.

    9 / 30
