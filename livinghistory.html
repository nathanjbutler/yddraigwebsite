Living History

As well as the fighting in Viking battles, we also have our own living history exhibit, a military encampment for a Viking unit on campaign, of portable tents and sea chests. This forms the backdrop to our public events, as well as providing our weekend home.

Here we live the Viking way for a few days, gathered around the central cooking fire and meet the public to educate about the history of the Viking Age (a subject we obviously find fascinating). In the encampment the public can meet Viking warriors, try on their armour and helmets, hold a shield and sword or maybe, if they are unlucky, be clamped in slave-chains, whilst around them craftsmen work, warriors repair and maintain weapons, a harpist plays and, over the fire, our evening meal cooks.

You can see us (and others) in action at our Facebook page - Friends of Y Ddraig and our Instagram Page - @yddraiggroup

Meet the Group!

  • Jarl Brodir Ulfrekson

    He command the longships that trade and raid across the Irish sea. He has allies and enemies in Ireland, Wales, England and the Western Isles.
    Photo credit goes to Russell Cobb

  • Elina Tomasdottir

    She's from the Viking longphort of Annagassan in Ireland, married to Jarl Konal of Mann, horsemaster and musician.

  • Yngvar Eriksson

    Of East Angle. His father was a boat builder and moved to Dublin to ply his trade. He grew up on the docks and decided he didn't want to join the family business and joined a ships crew for a life of adventure. He is now a seasoned warrior

  • Sven the Short

    Norwegian fishermen who got blown off course while fishing. They eventually landed on the east coast of Mercia, where they decided they didn't want to be fishermen anymore - they'd rather go on an adventure and become great warriors, and started to make their way across Mercia until they reached the Irish Sea. Deciding to see what was across it, they stole a boat, and ended up at the Isle of Mann, where they joined Jarl Brodir, which finally allowed them to be the proper warriors they always wanted to be.

  • Baggsy

    He's from Dublin, a trader for the jarl and a purveyor of cloth, furs, slaves and anything else he can get his hands on

  • Ulfar Sveinson

    His parents were farmers in Norway and he was a second born son who needed to go raiding if he was ever to find wealth and land. He travelled to Dublin in pursuit of this, where he found Jarl Brodir and became an oarsman/warrior. When not out raiding he enjoys tablet weaving

  • Sveinn Skovgaard

    Originally from Denmark, he's a former slave who fled to Dublin. He's a farmer, part of a ships crew and a wood worker

  • Ubbe Thorgilsson

    Son of hiberno-norse settler Thorgils who as a child was taken East to the lands of his mother on the Island of Bornholm, after his father had been banished following a change of local chieftain. Recently returned to Irish Sea Basin, looking to succeed where his father failed. Profession- warrior and Seidmann (sorcerer/seidr practitioner).

  • Konal Ospakr Ulfrekson

    He's the son of Jarl Ulfrek who hold a Quarterland farm on the Isle of Man. As a younger man he sailed his father longships on the spring and autumn "trip" but has been long abroad. He is a mercenary, raider and slave trader and has connection at courts from Wales and Winchester to Jorvik and Trondheim. He has been on many journeys and fought in many campaigns for many masters. He himself is a now a Jarl with his own ships but has not returned to the Isle of Man although rumours have come to him that after his fathers death his land has been usurped by his younger brother. One day that will be corrected!
